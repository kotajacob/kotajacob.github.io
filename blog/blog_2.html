Starting to learn C++

Posted on: 1/20/2016

Over the past few weeks I have begun a new venture in my programming career in an effort to get back into game development and start making games that I'm actually proud of. I'm learning C++. I started by getting the book “Programming Principles And Practice Using C++ 2nd Edition” by Bjarne Stroustrup the creator of C++. I found it to be extremely well written and full of great examples and challenges. Once I finish the book which should hopefully happen by the end of this month I want to move into doing some of the challenges on the /r/dailyprogrammer subreddit. If you haven't heard of this sub yet go check it out right now it has hundreds of great little challenges ranging from really simple like making tic tac toe to ones that would take an expert all day. Also if you are confused you can check the comments and see how others were able to solve it so you can get an idea of how you went wrong.

I think once I feel comfortable with C++ and can complete most of the easy and medium challenges without too much hair loss I'll start learning an engine because engines are great and writing big games that I want to make in C++ alone isn't really practical. The engine I'm looking at the most right now is Unreal Engine. I've downloaded and messed around with it a few times before and it uses C++ as the scripting language so that's a huge plus. Part of the reason I'm learning C++ and want to get Unreal instead of say Unity, the other big competitor I was looking into a bit, is that Unity still doesn't have a linux engine and I do most of my work on linux boxes these days and I'm not about to use a vm every time I want to do some game design. It's actually pretty surprising that they STILL don't have a linux engine yet considering they are well known for exporting to so many different devises and of course the whole being free thing probably helped with their fame.
